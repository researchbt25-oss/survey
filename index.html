We invite senior officers and above from the Ministry of Health, Ministry of Education and Skills Development, higher education institutions, and civil society organisations (excluding administrative, human resources, legal, and laboratory roles) to participate in this Royal University of Bhutan (RUB)-funded study on strengthening research-policy connections in Bhutan.

Effective policy making depends on evidence, yet the links between research and policy are often complex and underexplored. This study maps the engagement networks connecting policymakers, researchers, and intermediaries—such as civil society, media, and consultants—to understand how information flows, where collaboration thrives, and what barriers exist.

As a key member of this ecosystem, your insights will help reveal the relationships that drive evidence-based decision-making. Help us conduct quality research with your timely completion of this survey.

Participation is voluntary, with no right or wrong answers. All responses are strictly confidential; no individual names will appear in any published output, as the results will be reported only as aggregated network patterns.

Important — Specifying Contacts

When specifying contacts in Part 3, please write the full job title and organisation of each person (e.g., Director, Department of School Education, Ministry of Education, Skills and Development). Consistent and specific identification is essential for the analysis. Avoid vague descriptions such as "my boss" or "a colleague".
